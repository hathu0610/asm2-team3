a/ The function of chatbot

A chatbot (derived from a "chatbot") is a computer program that simulates a human conversation through voice or text communication (Salesforce 2021). The chatbot’s complexity can range from answering basic questions to virtual assistants that personalise responses as they learn and interact with users more. There are two types of chatbots: Task-oriented chatbots - handle simple questions, business tasks, and data-driven/predictive chatbots - sophisticated predictive analytics that enable personalization (Oracle 2020). Both types involve using NLP, Natural Language Processing in order to understand and talk in human language by text or oral speech with humans. NLP is a branch of AI with an aim to make computers comprehend and speak in the same manners as humans. For NLP to work, it combines rule-based modelling of human language with machine learning models (IBM 2020). To its full potential NLP can receive humans’ voices or text and comprehend our meaning, intent, and even emotion.
The first chatbot started in 1950 when Alan Turing - the father of computer science - came up with a question: “Can machines think” and since then the term “chatbot” was widely known (Adamopoulou 2020). After Turing’s questions, the first chatbot - Eliza - was made by Joseph Weizenbaum in 1966 with a ruled-base mechanism, which is still prevalent in modern days (Daffodil 2019). The next generation of chatbots opened with Perry and A.L.I.C.E in the 1990s, with the chatbot imitating real humans. The history of chatbots turned a new page in the 2000s when Siri and Cortana were introduced as conversational chatbots (Daffodil 2019).
As helpful as the chatbot may seem, humans might hold a different view of what chatbot is to them. The most prevalent view of chatbots is that they are productivity helpers, while some can be entertainers and companions (Adamopoulou 2020). The companion aspect came into the big picture when according to a study, more than 40% of customers request the businesses’ chatbots to be emotional and can detect humans’ sentiment like real human agents do (Adamopoulou 2020).
As per Global Market Insights, “The overall market size for chatbots worldwide would be over $1.3 billion by 2024.” (Global Market Insights 2019). As a result, it’s definite that the chatbot industry will witness some changes in the future. The state-of-the-art chatbot technology has many challenges: misunderstanding requests, executing inaccurate commands, and difficulty in understanding accents (Figure 8). In the future, chatbots are predicted to be more humanlike, with growth in NLP’s processing power.

Although this could result in a massive job loss, new job opportunities in tech areas increase around tech and bot builders. a/ The function of AI and machine learning

The concepts of artificial intelligence and machine learning are nothing novel nor new, in fact, the idea can be dated back decades and even before the 20th century. However, the rise of technological advancement over the century gives way to more powerful, flexible, diverse and complex compute resources, especially in the cloud, the volume and accessibility of digital data and the improvements in the performance and complexity of the machine, deep-learning algorithms are the main driving forces that allow for the quick growth of AI and machine learning in the recent decades(451 Research).
As of right now, with the help from the enormous digital database, both AI and machine learning can be implemented to solve real problems from a variety of different industries: facial recognition for security purposes, spotting spam emails for e-commerce providers, deploying drones to carry out contactless deliveries for online providers, etc (Joshi 2020). For example, one of the main focuses of Amazon when implementing AI into their systems is the search queries. By using an AI, Amazon is able to continuously understand their customers’ preferences and predict their customers’ search queries, therefore, providing a better customer experience by offering coupons that are related for each particular customer. And according to a report by Ekaterina Novoseltsev in 2018, the AI recommendation search engine drove 35% of Amazon’s total sales (Sejuti Das 2020). As for machine learning, it enables ideas like “self-driving car” to become a reality in recent years.
Companies like Tesla create and provide training models for the machine learning algorithm so that the car can identify objects, and respond accurately to the situation while driving, rather than relying on rigid, outdated or inaccurate data on the database. Using algorithms like SIFT (scale-invariant feature transform), AdaBoost, TextonBoost, Histogram of oriented gradients (HOG), YOLO (You Only Look Once), etc, machines can train themselves using fed data to further perfect their systems and thus creating an ideal program for self-driving cars (Mindy News Blog 2020).
Looking at the current state of AI and machine learning, we realize that those technologies are evolving at a rapid rate each year, constantly challenging their limitations and pushing their growth. Especially during the Covid-19 pandemic, when companies are struggling to adapt to online businesses, more and more enterprises are using AI and machine learning to reinforce and help with these changes.
As a result, the term “Federated machine learning” or “Cloud storage” will be more popular and occupy a more major role. The principles and techniques using federated machine learning mean that data no longer needs to be stored in the cloud. Today's devices can store more data than ever before, and probably more than users can produce. As a result, AI models that help to improve personalized services no longer need to rely on corporate servers and can instead reside on the device itself.
Similarly, the techniques used by integrated machine learning provide access to predictive AI models while ensuring that user data is stored on devices rather than servers. Data is also transmitted in a variety of ways. Advanced machine learning models used in federated machine learning keep data in the hands of data owners for greater privacy. This becomes an innovative approach to user privacy and a growing trend. A good example of this is Siri that works on the iPhone but doesn't send all of the user’s data to Apple's servers. Although federated machine learning and its principles are in use today, new commercial applications in this area are expected to emerge soon. For example, the advent of Apple's M1 chip and its industry-leading neural engine are designed specifically for advanced machine learning processes. Federated machine learning will have broader use in the financial services sector in areas such as credit risk prediction, and AI and machine learning applications will drive many other industries as well (Weissgraeber 2021).

a/ Cybersecurity's definition and its functions

Cybersecurity is making sure data, networks, and devices are not accessed by unauthorized people and so protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information (Cybersecurity & Infrastructure Security Agency 2009). Cybersecurity methods are, hence, applied to prevent computers, servers, networks, IT systems from malicious attacks. The reasons behind cyber attacks are various, they can be for financial gains, political information, or just to bring chaos to electronic systems (Kaspersky 2021).
Though there are no absolute solutions to cybersecurity and maintaining privacy, there are ways for people to better secure their online information. For personal security, conjoining individual awareness of cyber threats and cybersecurity software will yield the best results when safeguarding information. For businesses, defending against malicious attacks entails a mixture of information safety protocols, advanced technologies, and people’s awareness of information security (Gartner n.d).
As people move all of their data into the cloud in the work-from-home era, securing all of this data surely requires inconvenient multi-factor authentication. This explains the mass penetration of hackers into users' information as they use similar passwords across different sites for ease of access (Vizard, M 2021). Over the years, especially in such a rapid growth of IT operations, attackers are becoming more sophisticated with their technology, presenting a dire need for advanced cybersecurity. Technology such as Deepfakes, enhanced by Artificial Intelligence, can compromise even the most advanced systems (Help Net Security 2021). 5G is another considerably dangerous technology. With such an upsurge in download speed, hackers are capable of finding loopholes more easily and launching attacks at a greater scale (Singh 2021). It is clear that there are numerous emerging challenges to cybersecurity in the new era.
Several promising technologies have been introduced, shedding light on how to cope with the globally rising cyber threats. Distributed Ledger Technology presents a solution to the world by safeguarding Internet of Things systems. The applications of this technology are numerous, some of which are to verify transactions, reduce data silos, and improve data confidence (Fulmer 2021). For example, Blockchain, a branch of Distributed Ledger Technology, is taking the technology world by storm, especially in cybersecurity. Its decentralized nature helps systems become harder to penetrate (Singh 2021). In a few years, this technology ‘can be used to develop a standard security protocol, as it is a sounder alternative to end-to-end encryption,’ (Singh 2021), create decentralized administration, develop automatic data storage, which ultimately provides transparent and incorrupt data. Blockchain, therefore, has great potential in reducing cyber vulnerabilities during digital assets’ storing, accessing, and broadcasting.

b/ The impact of cybersecurity

According to PurpleSec’s cybersecurity statistics and trends, yearly, ransomware costs businesses around the world over 75 billion dollars (PurpleSec 2021). This data clearly shows how big an influence cyber attacks can have on businesses financially. Attacks aimed for business disruption affect businesses’ operations, in some cases, businesses suffer negligible loss, but in other cases, they might have to deal with grave profit loss. Even if the problem is financially negligible but persists for a long period of time, it is likely that the financial loss eventually amounts to a more significant number.
Beside financial setbacks, malicious cyber access to confidential business information can sabotage businesses’ reputation, leaving enormous, even fatal consequences for them to tackle. Information security gradually gains people’s attention so if a company cannot offer that to their customers, it is likely that they will experience loss of loyal customers, sales, and profit margin (Innefu 2021).
Technology has increasingly gained importance in people’s lives. With such a large amount of data, privacy breaches pose grave danger directly to technology users around the world. While it is clearly not easy for businesses, it is much harder for individuals, who do not have sufficient resources to recover from cyber attacks (ECPI University n.d). Witnessing the detrimental effects of cyber attacks, it is apparent that cybersecurity experts will become some of the most sought-after occupations shortly.

c/ Cybersecurity impact on daily life

It must be noted that anyone can be a target of malicious hackers, including myself. I, personally, have had a firsthand experience with an information breach when my social network account was hacked by email Phishing. Fortunately, I was able to regain access to this account and changed my passwords the day after the incident so that no huge repercussions took place. However, I am aware that there are many people, who are not as lucky as I was, suffering from cyber attackers’ activities. Knowing the various methods that hackers can use to retrieve my information and the consequences that follow forces me to educate myself and the people around me about cybersecurity.
Additionally, since it is known that even the largest conglomerates can fall prey to cyber attackers, users must be actively safeguarding their information for their own sake. Once personal information is leaked to the internet, it cannot be undone; that victim may have to deal with scams, identity theft, etc.

IV/ Project Idea

1. Overview

The project seeks to help RMIT students, parents, and faculty easily find answers or suggestions for their inquiries about the school through an FAQs chatbot. Instead of roaming the school’s website to try to figure out the answers by themselves, students can now conveniently use the chatbot, available on both Facebook Messenger and the website, to save their time and effort. The premade set of questions and answers is usually detailed and lengthy, as the university tries to provide the most complete answers to students, but the chatbot will summarize and break these answers down so that students and parents receive the quickest, most direct, most helpful responses. Not only will students benefit from this, RMIT staff benefit greatly from this chatbot as well. They may save a lot of resources thanks to the chatbot’s assistance in answering questions, focusing on delivering help, and tackling more difficult, complex questions from the RMIT community. With the help of Rasa Natural Language Processing, our chatbot will stand out in perceiving and clarifying users’ queries.

2. Motivation

New students at RMIT, having a lot of questions about the school, culture, classes, and support services, inspire the creation of a chatbot, one that provides well-prepared responses in a timely manner. There are more than 96,000 RMIT students globally (RMIT University 2021) so the number of questions coming from RMIT newbies and their family, will definitely surge at the beginning of each semester. By helping RMIT services from being overloaded, the FAQ chatbot is an ideal solution that reduces the cost for the school and saves everyone’s time. Statistics clearly show that chatbot implementation is rather beneficial for the university while not compromising overall users’ experience. According to Emilien (2019), there are over 1,200 business schools that have used FAQ chatbots to support students and received 99% of satisfaction from students with 63% of students who would like to experience the further integration of Chatbot into their classes (Nikoletta 2021). Besides, more than 30% of customer service costs are cut by using chatbots (WebsiteBuilder 2021).

3. Description

Botto is an advanced chatbot that has artificial intelligence (AI) capabilities whose main function is providing support via major text messaging applications and voice commands (Jake 2020). The detailed description of Botto is divided into three main sections: Botto’s skeleton, extended features, and integration.

Botto’s skeleton and its fundamental features

The content of the questions supporting RMIT new students will be set up based on the common questions collected on the RMIT website. For example, “due to the affection of Covid-19, what is the RMIT policy for students to return to campus?” RMIT provides many different categories of Frequently Asked Questions, for instance, two of which are “Return to campus” and “Vaccine requirements for students” (RMIT University 2021). Usually, to resolve their concerns, some students will find themselves scouring the school’s website into those FAQs sections, others sending requests to RMIT Connect. Botto chatbot will be the third, more handy source of information that students can turn to. Since RMIT has already prepared comprehensive answers on the website, the Botto chatbot team breaks down and categorizes this information into smaller, more concise responses. With Botto, not only will users receive text responses, but they will also receive visual representations such as photos, maps, videos for further clarification. In addition to responses, Botto’s ability to read users’ intentions is also advanced thanks to the team’s extensive research to enhance Natural Language Understanding technology. Botto has a personality unlike any other chatbots available in the market. The process of building Botto’s personality is based on the common qualities between team members (Personal information) which are amiable, polite, and funny. This type of personality can be applied to talk to any group of students at RMIT, keeping the conversation interesting while maintaining a suitable manner for RMIT’s professional working style. A chatbot with adaptable personalities is effective in making students feel more comfortable conversing with the chatbot and learning about the school, thus improving the user experience. Botto reflects the personality of users by imitating students’ characteristics through their chatting style (FPT AI 2020). Students can select at the beginning of their conversation their preferred style of conversation, or Botto can detect their mood and act accordingly later on. Emotions are then displayed visually through chat themes, emojis, and word choice. Thirdly, since it is inevitable that Botto will face questions out of its knowledge domain, students will have various alternatives to figure out the answers themselves. Botto will either show suggestions to websites that may contain answers to those questions or a button that requests live agents from RMIT who are capable of tackling more complex inquiries. During those instances, in order to expand Botto’s knowledge, the developer’s team will allow users to give instant feedback and suggest new sets of questions. This feedback is then documented for additional review and implementation.

Extended features

Setting reminders through emails and text messages. Botto sends emails to students, reminding them of upcoming events or tests. These emails are sent in the calendar format so that they are automatically added to students’ calendar
Checking room availability for booking Botto checks if, at a specific time, a room is available for booking or not, then allows students to book a room through the chat interface.
RMIT fun facts Aside from untangling students’ questions, Botto also has a feature that shows fun facts about RMIT to educate students about the school’s history and traditions.

Integration

For a complete experience of the chatbot, it is important that the chatbot can interact with people on different platforms. Especially when Botto is both text-based and voice-based, choosing a channel to run the chatbot on is as crucial as developing features. We believe that Facebook Messenger is the most suitable platform for the initial launch. This channel allows students to interact with Botto conveniently through both the familiar Messenger application and websites thanks to Facebook Messenger Chatbot Plugin for websites (Marketing Legal n.d). There are various reasons that our team chose to link Botto with Facebook Messenger App. The first reason is this app is in the top 10 of the most popular social media platforms that have many active users in 2021 (Statista 2021). Since Facebook is the most popular web application in the world with 2,895 million user access in 2021 (Figure 10), Facebook Messenger is a great means of communicating with students and delivering advertisements to future RMIT students. The second reason is the seamless conversation transfer from Facebook Messenger to RMIT webpage. This flexibility enables a one-stop experience for students and their families. The omnipresence of Messenger is, therefore, not only convenient to keep track of information and data but also easy for students to look up the answer later on (Marketing Legal n.d).
