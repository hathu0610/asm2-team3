II/ IT Work

Mr. Truong, Director of Digital Banking Technology at TPBank

1. IT work task

As the head of Director of Digital Banking Technology at TPBank, his day-to-day work involves meetings, long-term planning, and reporting. In the morning, he has a Daily Report Meeting with the Directors and C-Levels about the progress of the projects. After the Report Meeting, he holds his daily stand-up scrum according to agile methodology with his employees about their project progress and see if there are any problems. Stakeholder Meeting is also a vital part of his morning to identify the real problem that needs to be solved. For the rest of the day, he checks the status of the internet and the report about mobile banking to know if there are any bugs that need to be fixed and sends them to his team. With the urgent issue that accidentally occurs in the workplace, an unplanned meeting called Ad Hoc meetings will be scheduled immediately depending on the level situation.

2. The industry that interviewee works in

He works in the banking industry with many competitive competitors with the recent rise in digital banking improvement. He observes that, contrary to his previous banking company in Europe he worked for, the companies in the same field are not very competitive since each has its own individuality and advantages in the industry, while in Vietnam companies tend to copy each other. These results apply to all of the banking and fintech apps in Vietnam that have similar features even though some users might not need them.

3. Other types of work

Other kinds of work he has to do for his job mainly involve building reports: Progress Report and Product Roadmap. As the Director of Digital Banking at TP Bank, his product requires a long-term strategy with clear vision that stakeholders can align the goal when building the product - hence the importance of the Product Roadmap. The Product Roadmap mainly includes the product’s strategy, with smaller tactics for each sprint that the teams must complete. To ensure that the teams are following the Product Roadmap properly, each week/month the teams will have an update meeting to check the progress. Occasionally, he attends 2 meetings including the Progress Report of the product weekly and monthly and the Product Roadmap, which take around 2 to 3 hours. After each meeting, he gathers the information and conducts the progress report for his employers as well as reviewing their working status.

4. The interaction of the interviewee with different people in the workplace

In his job, he has to interact with different kinds of people, especially when his position holds many duties that involve coordination between many teams. He mainly communicates with clients, the head of directors, my in-house teams, and outsource teams. Since TPBank is a fast-growing bank, it has collaborated with many big corporations and clients. As a result, he often has meetings with those clients in order to understand what they need and their expectations for my teams. After understanding what his clients want, he needs to communicate his client’s needs to his team, including both in-house and outsourced employees. Since many of his projects are simultaneously active, and each of which is of giant scope, his team gets divided into 12 smaller teams consisting of 5 to 7 members each.

5. The interactions of the interviewee with other IT professionals.

As mentioned above, he has to interact with 3 different types of IT professionals: the head of directors, his in-house teams, and the outsource team. With the head of directors, they occasionally have two-way discussions about the future of the product, and debate about which strategies should they implement next. With his in-house teams, they have both formal meetings about the team’s progress, as well as casual chats to learn about each other’s lives outside work. It’s not the same with his outsource team since they do not have many opportunities to interact face-to-face and thus, their interactions occur only once a week with formal updates about the outsource team’s working status.

6. The interactions of the interviewee with clients

When interacting with clients, his interaction is strictly professional and the topic of conversations are mainly about their expectations for the project. This can be a great learning opportunity to understand what clients think about the product and what can still be improved. Although the goal of the interactions are about gathering feedback from the clients, preparation is a vital part since it shows the clients how professional his companies are. After he receives a problem from the clients, he also makes sure that his team follows up with the clients after the problem is solved. The first few interactions are of vital importance since it could be the foundation for a strong, long-term cooperation.

7. Aspects of work that spend most of the time on

Since he holds the position of Director of Digital Banking Technology at TPBank, he is responsible for attending different meetings with his team to hear reports from recent situations or going to clients’ companies to collaborate with or make a deal with his clients every day. Before Covid, this forced him to spend most of his time on vehicles commuting between different locations to attend various meetings with different people. However, after covid, his face-to-face meetings are changed to online meetings in different channels using Microsoft Team, since offline meetings are restricted and the company’s employees, including his team, are allowed to work hybrid. The restriction acts as a blessing in disguise since later in the interview, he shared that this restriction allows everybody to recognize the benefits of working online through Team, especially when he has multiple meetings to attend to in a day. Not only does this allow the company to save money on commuting expenses but also helps Mr.Truong in saving him a lot of time, allowing him to work more effectively and efficiently. Therefore, because of Covid, nowadays, he spends most of his time in his office dealing with reports and meetings.

8. The challenging work task

Surprisingly, according to Mr.Truong, the most challenging aspect of his work is also the most enjoyable aspect for him in his job. He is afraid of how fast technology is evolving every day and worries that one day he isn’t going to catch up with newer and more advanced technologies. However, throughout his IT career, he was constantly assigned and exposed to those newer technologies, making him enjoy working with new and unfamiliar technologies. Looking back at his career, he stated that he had been very lucky since every time he was bored of working with old technology, he would be moved to another department that was researching a new technology with different, exciting teams.

9. The essence of the IT industry

Since he is in a manager position of the company, his daily work doesn’t require him to be actively involved in working with the technology but rather checking status from his team and attending meetings for current and future projects. Every morning, he and his team would hold a meeting to discuss current progress in their work and devise a plan to solve issues that they are dealing with. Although there isn’t any technical issue that requires him to personally fix, he uses his personal experience to guide his team when problems relating to the team’s new technology arise, which minimizes the effect of the problems while also providing his team with valuable insight in problem-solving skills. Furthermore, with his abundant knowledge in IT, he would be able to simplify IT concepts and give out effective speeches in his meeting with the chief information officer and board of directors, convincing them to use more innovative approaches to current problems inside the company.

III/ IT Technologies

a/ The function of chatbot

A chatbot (derived from a "chatbot") is a computer program that simulates a human conversation through voice or text communication (Salesforce 2021). The chatbot’s complexity can range from answering basic questions to virtual assistants that personalise responses as they learn and interact with users more. There are two types of chatbots: Task-oriented chatbots - handle simple questions, business tasks, and data-driven/predictive chatbots - sophisticated predictive analytics that enable personalization (Oracle 2020). Both types involve using NLP, Natural Language Processing in order to understand and talk in human language by text or oral speech with humans. NLP is a branch of AI with an aim to make computers comprehend and speak in the same manners as humans. For NLP to work, it combines rule-based modelling of human language with machine learning models (IBM 2020). To its full potential NLP can receive humans’ voices or text and comprehend our meaning, intent, and even emotion.
As per Global Market Insights, “The overall market size for chatbots worldwide would be over $1.3 billion by 2024.” (Global Market Insights 2019). As a result, it’s definite that the chatbot industry will witness some changes in the future. The state-of-the-art chatbot technology has many challenges: misunderstanding requests, executing inaccurate commands, and difficulty in understanding accents (Figure 8). In the future, chatbots are predicted to be more humanlike, with growth in NLP’s processing power.

b/ The impact of chatbot

Chatbot adoption is on the rise: since 2018, service organisations have increased their use of chatbots - often powered by artificial intelligence (AI) - by nearly two-thirds (Salesforce 2020). Where there is communication through natural language, there is a possibility for the application of chatbots. Some impacts of chatbots include providing educational content and e-learning practises, 24/7 customer service for e-commerce businesses, and customised health information for patients in health care. With the heavy impact of Covid, B2C and B2B companies are even more prone to apply chatbots, as 73% said it affected the way they interact with customers (FPT AI 2021). The top benefits of chatbots in business include reducing operating costs, improving agency efficiency, and having a deeper understanding of customer needs (FPT AI 2021). In the future, the chatbot is expected to replace menial jobs, especially in the customer service industry. At customer service centres, chatbots work 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, while managing many customers at the same time and reducing payroll costs. Although this could result in a massive job loss, new job opportunities in tech areas increase around tech and bot builders. Companies that used to be call-centre with human customer service only see a rise of 15% in pay when they became bot builders (Time 2020).

c/ The affection of chatbot individually

Personally, the rise of chatbots has already affected me and I deem that it will have a greater effect in the future. In my daily life, chatbot has assisted in many areas: online shopping, digital banking and education. The most positive encounter I had with chatbots was when my credit card was stolen, I chatted with the bank and the chatbot assisted me to close my card in 5 minutes without me having to contact a human agent. With speech-to-text technology integrated with chatbot, my family members - especially the older generation - will benefit greatly since they are not good with technology and can receive assistance through voice bot/chatbot.
